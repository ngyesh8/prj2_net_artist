TURUX

  • Year: 1995-1997; Rebuilt 2020-Present
  • Medium: Interactive Generative Art (p5js, formerly Director)
  • Exhibitions: Centre Pompidou, Paris (2003). Künstlerhaus, Vienna (2000)
  • Award: Design Austria, 1998

Turux, first launched in 1997, is a series of 21 semi-automatic artworks originally created for CD-ROM distribution and presented in their classic 800×600 pixel resolution. The project was born out of collaboration, with LIA’s contribution focusing on algorithmic design and code-based interaction that explored the possibilities of art on the early web. Since it was among the first examples of internet-based generative art, TURUX gained international recognition, featured in books, magazines, and awarded by Design Austria in 1998.

Emerging during the rise of net art and digital experimentation in the late 1990s, the project’s influence continued as LIA later rebuilt the entire series in p5.js, ensuring its accessibility for modern browsers and preservation within digital art history. All pieces of the work was originally developed using Macromedia Director, a discontinued software that was popularly used for creating interactive software known as Shockwave files.
